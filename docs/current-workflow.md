# Current Business Workflow (As-Is)

## Purpose

This document describes the current business workflow used by the event hall owner to manage reservations before the implementation of the new management system.

The objective is to understand the current process, identify its limitations, and establish a baseline for designing the new application.

---

# Workflow

## 1. Customer Acquisition

Most customers are acquired through Facebook groups where people publish posts looking for an event hall.

The owner reviews each publication and evaluates whether the requested event fits the hall capacity, which is approximately 60 to 80 guests.

If the request is suitable, the owner replies using a predefined promotional message that includes:

- Facebook page
- Hall description
- Available services
- Capacity
- Price
- Contact phone numbers

After the initial reply, photographs of the event hall are sent to the potential customer.

If the customer remains interested, the conversation usually continues through Facebook Messenger, WhatsApp or phone calls.

---

## 2. Availability Check

The customer usually asks whether a specific date is available.

The owner manually checks an Excel spreadsheet containing all reservations.

If the requested date is already reserved, the customer is informed and another date may be suggested.

If the date is available, the reservation process continues.

---

## 3. Hall Visit

Before making a reservation, most customers schedule a visit to the event hall.

The purpose of the visit is to inspect the facilities and determine whether the venue meets their expectations.

Whenever possible, visits are scheduled during another event so potential customers can see the venue already decorated.

---

## 4. Reservation

If the customer decides to rent the venue, a deposit is requested.

Once the deposit has been received, the reservation is manually recorded in the Excel spreadsheet.

The reservation becomes officially confirmed after the deposit payment.

---

## 5. Event Preparation

On the morning of the event, the owner opens the hall so the customer can decorate the venue.

After the decoration has been completed, the hall is closed again until the agreed start time.

---

## 6. Event Start

At the agreed event start time, the owner opens the hall again.

Before handing over the venue, the remaining balance is collected from the customer.

Once the payment has been completed, the customer is allowed to use the venue for the duration of the event.

---

## 7. Event End

Near the scheduled ending time, the owner returns to the venue.

A general inspection is performed to verify that:

- Furniture has not been damaged.
- Equipment is complete.
- Nothing has been stolen.
- The facilities remain in good condition.

---

## 8. Cleanup

After the customers leave, the owner cleans the venue.

Typical cleanup activities include:

- Collecting tablecloths.
- Washing reusable materials.
- Cleaning the hall.
- Verifying furniture and equipment.
- Preparing the venue for the next reservation.

---

# Current Tools

The business currently relies on the following tools:

- Facebook Groups for customer acquisition.
- Facebook Messenger for customer communication.
- WhatsApp for customer communication.
- Phone calls.
- Microsoft Excel for reservation management.

---

# Current Challenges

The current workflow presents several limitations:

- Reservation information is stored manually in Excel.
- Customer conversations are distributed across multiple communication channels.
- Availability checks are completely manual.
- Customer special requirements are difficult to organize.
- Repetitive messages must be written or copied manually.
- There is no centralized customer history.
- Payments require manual tracking.
- General reminders and maintenance tasks are managed outside the reservation process.

These limitations increase the risk of human error and make the reservation process more time-consuming.
